Hexagon

Six sides — the shape bees build with

What Is It?

A hexagon is a flat shape with 6 straight sides and 6 corners. In a regular hexagon all sides match and every angle is 120°.

Honeycomb cells, many floor tiles, nuts and bolts seen from above, and snowflake arms are hexagons.

Properties

A regular hexagon needs one number: the side length s.

Sides6 — all equal if regular
Corners6
Each angle120°
All angles add up to720°
Lines of symmetry6
Diagonals9
Tiles a flat floor?Yes — with no gaps at all

Formulas

Area A = (3√3⁄2)s²

Perimeter P = 6s

Let's Try It!

Take a regular hexagon with side s = 4.

A = (3√3⁄2) × 16 = 24√3 ≈ 41.57

P = 6s = 24

Cool Fact

Bees use hexagons because they fit together perfectly with the least wax for the most honey. Mathematicians only proved this was the best possible plan in 1999.
