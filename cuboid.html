Cuboid

A stretched box — like a shoebox

What Is It?

A cuboid (also called a rectangular prism) has 6 rectangular faces that meet at square corners. Unlike a cube, its length, width and height can all be different.

Cereal boxes, bricks, phones and books are cuboids. A cube is simply a cuboid where every side happens to be equal.

Properties

A cuboid needs three numbers: length l, width w and height h.

Faces6 — all rectangles
Edges12 — in 3 groups of 4
Corners (vertices)8
Faces meeting at each corner3
Flat or curvedAll flat
Lines of symmetry3 planes of symmetry
Slice it across and you getA rectangle
Euler's rule8 − 12 + 6 = 2 ✓

Formulas

Volume V = l × w × h

Surface area SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)

Space diagonal D = √(l² + w² + h²)

Let's Try It!

Take a cuboid with l = 5, w = 3, h = 4.

V = 5 × 3 × 4 = 60

SA = 2(15 + 20 + 12) = 94

D = √(25 + 9 + 16) = √50 ≈ 7.07

Cool Fact

Nobody has ever found a "perfect cuboid" — one where every edge, every face diagonal and the space diagonal are all whole numbers. Mathematicians still don't know if one exists.
