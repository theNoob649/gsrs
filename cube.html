Cube

A box with six equal square sides

What Is It?

A cube is a solid with six square faces that are all exactly the same size. Every edge is the same length and every corner is a square corner (a right angle). That makes the cube one of the most perfectly even shapes there is — a dice is a cube you probably already know.

Cubes are everywhere in daily life: gaming dice, sugar cubes, Rubik's cubes, cardboard boxes that happen to be square, and the building blocks children stack are all cubes or very close to it.

Parts & Sizes

The cube is defined by a single measurement — its side length s.

Faces6 (all squares)
Edges12
Vertices8
Side lengths

Like every flat-sided shape, it follows Euler's rule — corners − edges + faces = 8 − 12 + 6 = 2.

Formulas

Volume V = s³

Surface area SA = 6s²

Face diagonal d = s√2

Space diagonal D = s√3

Let's Try It!

Take a cube with side s = 4.

V = s³ = 4³ = 64

SA = 6s² = 6 × 4² = 6 × 16 = 96

Face diagonal = s√2 = 4√2 ≈ 5.66

Space diagonal = s√3 = 4√3 ≈ 6.93

Cool Fact

The cube is the only Platonic solid that tiles 3D space perfectly with no gaps — which is exactly why bricks, shipping containers, and warehouse shelving are all built on cuboid grids.
