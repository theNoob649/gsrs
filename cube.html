Cube

A box with six equal square sides

What Is It?

A cube is a solid with 6 square faces that are all exactly the same size. Every edge is the same length and every corner is a square corner (a right angle).

Dice, sugar cubes, Rubik's cubes and wooden building blocks are all cubes.

Properties

A cube needs just one number: the side length s.

Faces6 — all squares
Edges12 — all the same length
Corners (vertices)8
Faces meeting at each corner3
Flat or curvedAll flat — no curved surfaces
Lines of symmetry9 planes of symmetry
Slice it across and you getA square
Euler's rule8 − 12 + 6 = 2 ✓

Formulas

Volume V = s³

Surface area SA = 6s²

Face diagonal d = s√2

Space diagonal D = s√3

Let's Try It!

Take a cube with side s = 4.

V = s³ = 64 → 64

SA = 6s² = 6 × 16 = 96

Face diagonal = 4√2 ≈ 5.66

Space diagonal = 4√3 ≈ 6.93

Cool Fact

Cubes stack together with no gaps at all. That's why bricks, boxes and shipping containers are box-shaped — nothing gets wasted.
