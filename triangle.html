Triangle

Three sides, three corners — the strongest shape

What Is It?

A triangle is a flat shape with 3 straight sides and 3 corners. No matter what it looks like, its three angles always add up to 180°.

Triangles come in types: equilateral (all 3 sides equal), isosceles (2 sides equal), and scalene (all different). A right triangle has one square corner.

Properties

To find the area you need the base (b) and the height (h) — the height is measured straight up from the base.

Sides3
Corners3
All angles add up to180°
Equilateral triangle3 equal sides, every angle 60°, 3 lines of symmetry
Isosceles triangle2 equal sides, 1 line of symmetry
Scalene triangleNo equal sides, no lines of symmetry
Diagonals0 — a triangle has none

Formulas

Area A = ½ × b × h

Perimeter P = a + b + c

Equilateral area A = (√3⁄4)a²

Let's Try It!

Take a triangle with base b = 10 and height h = 6.

A = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30

Now an equilateral triangle with side a = 6:

A = (√3⁄4) × 36 ≈ 15.59

Cool Fact

A triangle can't be pushed out of shape without bending a side, which is why bridges, roofs, and cranes are full of them.
