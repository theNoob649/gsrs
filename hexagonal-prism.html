Hexagonal Prism

A tube with six-sided ends — like a pencil

What Is It?

A hexagonal prism has two matching hexagon ends joined by 6 rectangles. Every slice across it is the same hexagon.

Ordinary pencils, the nuts that screw onto bolts, and the cells of a honeycomb are hexagonal prisms.

Properties

A regular hexagonal prism needs two numbers: the base side a and the height h.

Faces8 — 2 hexagons + 6 rectangles
Edges18
Corners (vertices)12
Bases2 matching hexagons
Flat or curvedAll flat
Slice it across and you getA hexagon
Euler's rule12 − 18 + 8 = 2 ✓

Formulas

Base area B = (3√3⁄2)a²

Base perimeter P = 6a

Volume V = B × h

Side surface LSA = 6ah

Surface area SA = 2B + 6ah

Let's Try It!

Take a hexagonal prism with a = 4 and h = 10.

B = (3√3⁄2) × 16 = 24√3 ≈ 41.57

V = B × h ≈ 415.69

SA = 2B + 6ah = 48√3 + 240 ≈ 323.14

Cool Fact

Pencils are hexagonal for a very practical reason: a round pencil rolls off the desk, but a six-sided one stops after a small nudge.
