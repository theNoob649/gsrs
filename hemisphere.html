Hemisphere

Half of a ball — a dome

What Is It?

A hemisphere is what you get when you cut a sphere exactly through the middle. You are left with a curved dome and one flat circle underneath.

Domes on buildings, bowls, and half an orange are hemispheres.

Properties

A hemisphere needs just one number: the radius r.

Flat faces1 — the circle at the bottom
Curved surfaces1 dome
Edges1 curved edge (the rim)
Corners (vertices)0
Lines of symmetryEndless vertical planes through the middle
Slice it across and you getA circle
Compared to a sphereExactly half the volume

Formulas

Volume V = (2⁄3)πr³

Curved surface CSA = 2πr²

Surface area SA = 3πr² (dome + flat circle)

Let's Try It!

Take a hemisphere with radius r = 3.

V = (2⁄3)π × 27 = 18π ≈ 56.55

CSA = 2π × 9 = 18π ≈ 56.55

SA = 3π × 9 = 27π ≈ 84.82

Cool Fact

A dome is incredibly strong because it passes weight smoothly down its curve, letting builders roof enormous rooms without a single pillar in the way.
