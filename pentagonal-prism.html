Pentagonal Prism

A tube with five-sided ends

What Is It?

A pentagonal prism has two matching pentagon ends joined by 5 rectangles. Every slice across it is the same pentagon.

The Pentagon building in America is a very short, very wide pentagonal prism. Some pencils and bolts use this shape too.

Properties

A regular pentagonal prism needs two numbers: the base side a and the height h.

Faces7 — 2 pentagons + 5 rectangles
Edges15
Corners (vertices)10
Bases2 matching pentagons
Flat or curvedAll flat
Slice it across and you getA pentagon
Euler's rule10 − 15 + 7 = 2 ✓

Formulas

Base area B ≈ 1.72 × a²

Base perimeter P = 5a

Volume V = B × h

Side surface LSA = 5ah

Surface area SA = 2B + 5ah

Let's Try It!

Take a pentagonal prism with a = 4 and h = 10.

B ≈ 1.72 × 16 ≈ 27.53

V = B × h ≈ 275.28

SA = 2B + 5ah ≈ 55.06 + 200 ≈ 255.06

Cool Fact

The Pentagon is one of the biggest office buildings on Earth, yet its five-sided design means you can walk between any two of its farthest points in about seven minutes.
