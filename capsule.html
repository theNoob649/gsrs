Capsule

A cylinder capped by two hemispheres

Description

A capsule (or stadium of revolution) is a cylinder with a hemisphere attached to each end. The two rounded caps share the cylinder's radius r, so the whole surface is perfectly smooth. Its measurements are the radius r and the straight cylindrical height h (the length of the middle section only, not counting the caps).

Real-world capsules include medicine pills, propane and diving gas tanks, and the pods in some amusement-park rides.

Properties & Measurements

A capsule is a curved solid defined by the radius r and the cylinder height h.

Surfaces1 curved cylindrical side + 2 hemispherical caps
Radiusr
Cylinder heighth (straight middle section)
Total lengthh + 2r

Formulas

Volume V = πr²h + (4⁄3)πr³

Surface area SA = 2πrh + 4πr²

Worked Example

Take a capsule with radius r = 2 and cylinder height h = 6.

V = πr²h + (4⁄3)πr³ = π(4)(6) + (4⁄3)π(8) = 24π + (32⁄3)π ≈ 108.91

SA = 2πrh + 4πr² = 2π(2)(6) + 4π(4) = 24π + 16π = 40π ≈ 125.66

Fun Fact

The two hemispherical caps of a capsule together make one complete sphere — which is why the capsule's volume is just a cylinder plus a single full sphere, (4⁄3)πr³.
