Cone

A round base that rises to a point

What Is It?

A cone has a circle at the bottom and one curved surface that sweeps up to a single point on top, called the apex.

Ice-cream cones, traffic cones, party hats and funnels are all cones.

Properties

You need the radius r and the height h. The slant height l runs along the outside from the apex down to the rim.

Flat faces1 — the circular base
Curved surfaces1
Edges1 curved edge (the rim)
Corners (vertices)1 — the point on top
Slice it across and you getA circle
Euler's ruleDoesn't apply — a cone isn't made of flat faces

Formulas

Slant height l = √(r² + h²)

Volume V = (1⁄3)πr²h

Curved surface LSA = πrl

Surface area SA = πr(r + l)

Let's Try It!

Take a cone with r = 3 and h = 4.

l = √(9 + 16) = 5

V = (1⁄3)π × 9 × 4 = 12π ≈ 37.70

SA = π × 3 × (3 + 5) = 24π ≈ 75.40

Cool Fact

A cone holds exactly one third of the cylinder with the same base and height — so it takes three full cones of water to fill a matching can.
