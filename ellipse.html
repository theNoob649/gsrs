Ellipse

A circle that has been stretched — an oval

What Is It?

An ellipse is a squashed or stretched circle. Instead of one radius it has two: a long one (a) and a short one (b). If the two are equal, the ellipse turns back into a circle.

Running tracks, some mirrors, and the shape of an egg from the side are all close to ellipses.

Properties

An ellipse needs two numbers: the long radius a and the short radius b.

Straight sides0 (one smooth curved edge)
Corners0
Lines of symmetry2 — one across, one up and down
Long radiusa
Short radiusb
Special caseIf a = b it becomes a circle

Formulas

Area A = πab

Perimeter C ≈ π[ 3(a+b) − √((3a+b)(a+3b)) ]

Let's Try It!

Take an ellipse with a = 6 and b = 4.

A = πab = π × 6 × 4 ≈ 75.4

C ≈ π[3(10) − √(22 × 18)] ≈ 31.7

Cool Fact

Planets don't travel in circles — they travel in ellipses, with the Sun sitting slightly off-centre. Johannes Kepler worked this out over 400 years ago.
