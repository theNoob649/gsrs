Did You Know?

Every convex polyhedron on this site obeys Euler's formula, one of the most elegant results in geometry:

V − E + F = 2

Count the vertices (V), subtract the edges (E), add the faces (F), and you always land on 2 — whether it's a humble cube (8 − 12 + 6) or a 20-faced icosahedron (12 − 30 + 20). Try it on any flat-faced shape in the list above.
