Sphere

A perfectly round ball

What Is It?

A sphere is a solid where every single point on the outside is exactly the same distance from the middle. That distance is the radius.

Basketballs, marbles, bubbles and planets are spheres (or very nearly).

Properties

A sphere needs just one number: the radius r.

Flat faces0
Curved surfaces1 smooth surface
Edges0
Corners (vertices)0
Lines of symmetryEndless — any line through the middle
Slice it anywhere and you getA circle, every single time
Special propertyThe only shape that looks identical from every direction

Formulas

Volume V = (4⁄3)πr³

Surface area SA = 4πr²

Diameter d = 2r

Circle around the middle C = 2πr

Let's Try It!

Take a sphere with radius r = 3.

V = (4⁄3)π × 27 = 36π ≈ 113.10

SA = 4π × 9 = 36π ≈ 113.10

d = 2r = 6

Cool Fact

Out of every possible shape holding the same amount inside, the sphere has the smallest skin. Bubbles turn into spheres all by themselves for exactly that reason.
