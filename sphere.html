Sphere

A perfectly round curved solid

Description

A sphere is the set of all points in space that are the same distance — the radius — from a single center point. It is perfectly round in every direction, with no faces, no edges, and no vertices, and it encloses the greatest possible volume for a given surface area.

Everyday spheres include basketballs, marbles, ball bearings, soap bubbles, and — very nearly — planets and stars, which gravity pulls into round shapes.

Properties & Measurements

A sphere is a curved solid defined entirely by its radius r.

Faces / Edges / Verticesnone (a single smooth surface)
Radiusr (center to surface)
Diameter2r (across, through the center)

Formulas

Volume V = (4⁄3)πr³

Surface area SA = 4πr²

Diameter d = 2r

Great circle C = 2πr

Worked Example

Take a sphere with radius r = 3.

V = (4⁄3)πr³ = (4⁄3)π(27) = 36π ≈ 113.10

SA = 4πr² = 4π(9) = 36π ≈ 113.10

Diameter = 2r = 6

Great-circle circumference = 2πr = 6π ≈ 18.85

Fun Fact

Of all shapes that enclose a given volume, the sphere has the smallest surface area. That's why bubbles and water droplets pull themselves into spheres — surface tension is minimizing area automatically.
