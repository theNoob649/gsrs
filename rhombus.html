Rhombus

A diamond — four equal sides

What Is It?

A rhombus is a flat shape with 4 sides that are all the same length, but the corners are usually not square. It looks like a diamond or a squashed square.

You'll see rhombus shapes in kites, playing-card diamonds, and lots of tile patterns.

Properties

A rhombus can be measured with its two diagonals (d₁ and d₂) or with a base and height.

Sides4 — all equal
Corners4
Parallel sides2 pairs
Opposite anglesEqual to each other
All angles add up to360°
Lines of symmetry2 — along each diagonal
Diagonals2 — they cross at right angles and cut each other in half
Also known asA parallelogram with all sides equal

Formulas

Area (diagonals) A = (d₁ × d₂) ⁄ 2

Area (base) A = b × h

Perimeter P = 4s

Let's Try It!

Take a rhombus with diagonals d₁ = 10 and d₂ = 6, and side s = 5.

A = (10 × 6) ⁄ 2 = 30

P = 4s = 20

Cool Fact

Every square is a rhombus, but most rhombuses are not squares — a square is simply a rhombus that decided to have right angles.
