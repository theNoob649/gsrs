Parallelogram

A pushed-over rectangle

What Is It?

A parallelogram is a flat shape with 4 sides where both pairs of opposite sides are parallel and equal. It looks like a rectangle that someone leaned over to one side.

You can spot them in scissor lifts, some tile patterns, and the shape a rectangle casts as a shadow.

Properties

Use the base (b) and the height (h). Careful: the height is the straight-up distance, not the slanted side.

Sides4 — opposite sides equal
Corners4
Parallel sides2 pairs
Opposite anglesEqual to each other
All angles add up to360°
Lines of symmetry0 (for a slanted one)
Diagonals2 — they cut each other exactly in half

Formulas

Area A = b × h

Perimeter P = 2(a + b)

Let's Try It!

Take a parallelogram with base b = 9, height h = 4 and slanted side a = 5.

A = b × h = 9 × 4 = 36

P = 2(5 + 9) = 28

Cool Fact

Slide the slanted triangle from one end of a parallelogram to the other and you get a rectangle — that's exactly why its area is base × height.
