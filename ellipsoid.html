Ellipsoid

A stretched ball — like an egg

What Is It?

An ellipsoid is a smooth, closed surface that looks like a sphere stretched or squashed along its axes. It is described by three semi-axes — a, b, and c — the distances from the center to the surface along each of the three perpendicular directions. When all three are equal (a = b = c) the ellipsoid is simply a sphere.

Real-world ellipsoids include a rugby or American football, an egg (roughly), M&M candies, and — on a planetary scale — the Earth itself, which bulges slightly at the equator.

Parts & Sizes

An ellipsoid is a curved solid defined by three semi-axes a, b, c.

Surface1 smooth curved surface
Semi-axesa, b, c (center to surface along each axis)
Special casea = b = c → sphere

Formulas

Volume V = (4⁄3)πabc

Surface area SA ≈ 4π · [ ( aᵖbᵖ + aᵖcᵖ + bᵖcᵖ ) ⁄ 3 ](1⁄p)

with p ≈ 1.6075  (Thomsen approximation)

Unlike the sphere, an ellipsoid has no simple exact formula for surface area; the Thomsen formula above is a close approximation. Setting a = b = c recovers the exact sphere result, SA = 4πr².

Let's Try It!

Take an ellipsoid with semi-axes a = 3, b = 2, c = 1.

V = (4⁄3)πabc = (4⁄3)π(3)(2)(1) = 8π ≈ 25.13

With p = 1.6075: aᵖ ≈ 5.847, bᵖ ≈ 3.048, cᵖ = 1

(aᵖbᵖ + aᵖcᵖ + bᵖcᵖ)⁄3 = (17.82 + 5.85 + 3.05)⁄3 ≈ 8.904

SA ≈ 4π × 8.904(1⁄1.6075) ≈ 4π × 3.897 ≈ 48.97

Cool Fact

Earth is not a perfect sphere but an oblate ellipsoid — spinning makes it bulge at the equator, so the planet is about 43 km wider across the equator than from pole to pole.
