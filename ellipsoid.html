Ellipsoid

A stretched ball — like an egg

What Is It?

An ellipsoid is a sphere that has been stretched or squashed. Instead of one radius it has three, called a, b and c — one for each direction.

Rugby balls, eggs and M&M sweets are ellipsoid shapes. If all three radii are equal, it turns back into a sphere.

Properties

An ellipsoid needs three numbers: the semi-axes a, b and c.

Flat faces0
Curved surfaces1 smooth surface
Edges0
Corners (vertices)0
Lines of symmetry3 planes of symmetry
Slice it and you getA circle or an ellipse
Special caseIf a = b = c it is a sphere

Formulas

Volume V = (4⁄3)πabc

Surface area SA ≈ 4π[ (aᵢbᵢ + aᵢcᵢ + bᵢcᵢ) ⁄ 3 ](1⁄p), p ≈ 1.6075

Let's Try It!

Take an ellipsoid with a = 3, b = 2, c = 1.

V = (4⁄3)π × 3 × 2 × 1 = 8π ≈ 25.13

SA ≈ 48.97 (there is no simple exact formula)

Cool Fact

The Earth is not a perfect sphere — spinning makes it bulge, so it is about 43 km wider around the equator than from pole to pole.
