Cylinder

A round tube with flat ends — like a can

What Is It?

A cylinder has two matching circles — one at the top, one at the bottom — joined by one curved surface wrapped around the outside.

Drink cans, candles, batteries, pipes and drinking glasses are cylinders.

Properties

A cylinder needs two numbers: the radius r and the height h.

Flat faces2 — the circles on the ends
Curved surfaces1 wrapped around the side
Edges2 curved edges (the rims)
Corners (vertices)0
Bases2 matching circles
Slice it across and you getA circle
Euler's ruleDoesn't apply — it has curved surfaces

Formulas

Volume V = πr²h

Curved surface CSA = 2πrh

Surface area SA = 2πr(r + h)

Circle around the base C = 2πr

Let's Try It!

Take a cylinder with r = 3 and h = 7.

V = π × 9 × 7 = 63π ≈ 197.92

CSA = 2π × 3 × 7 = 42π ≈ 131.95

SA = 2π × 3 × 10 = 60π ≈ 188.50

Cool Fact

Peel the label off a can and flatten it out and you get a perfect rectangle. Its width is the circle around the can, which is why the curved surface is 2πr × h.
