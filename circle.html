Circle

A perfectly round flat shape

What Is It?

A circle is a flat shape made of all the points that sit exactly the same distance from the middle. That distance is the radius. Go straight across through the middle and you have the diameter, which is always twice the radius.

You see circles everywhere: wheels, coins, clock faces, buttons, and a pizza before anyone cuts it.

Properties

A circle is described by just one number — its radius r.

Straight sides0 (one smooth curved edge)
Corners0
Lines of symmetryEndless — every line through the middle
Radiusr — middle to edge
Diameterd = 2r — all the way across
Special propertyEvery point on the edge is the same distance from the centre

Formulas

Area A = πr²

Perimeter C = 2πr = πd

Diameter d = 2r

Let's Try It!

Take a circle with radius r = 5.

A = πr² = π × 25 ≈ 78.5

C = 2πr = 2π × 5 ≈ 31.4

Cool Fact

The number π (about 3.14) never stops and never repeats. Computers have worked it out to trillions of digits and still haven't found a pattern.
