Square Pyramid

A square base that rises to a point

What Is It?

A square pyramid has a square bottom and 4 triangles that lean in and meet at a single point on top, called the apex.

The Great Pyramids of Egypt are the most famous ones. You'll also see the shape in tent tops and some roofs.

Properties

You need the base side a and the height h. The slant height l runs up the middle of a triangle face.

Faces5 — 1 square + 4 triangles
Edges8
Corners (vertices)5 — 4 at the base, 1 apex on top
Bases1 square
Flat or curvedAll flat
Lines of symmetry4 planes of symmetry
Slice it across and you getA smaller square
Euler's rule5 − 8 + 5 = 2 ✓

Formulas

Slant height l = √(h² + (a⁄2)²)

Volume V = (1⁄3)a²h

Side surface LSA = 2a × l

Surface area SA = a² + 2a × l

Let's Try It!

Take a square pyramid with a = 6 and h = 4.

l = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5

V = (1⁄3) × 36 × 4 = 48

SA = 36 + 2 × 6 × 5 = 96

Cool Fact

The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tallest building humans had ever made for about 3,800 years — no other record in history has lasted anywhere near as long.
